    This mat of 3,000 little spikes feels as scary as it looks. I laid on it for the first time without a shirt, and my brain immediately panicked because the pain/pressure/burning sensation was too intense. I had read previous reviews that mentioned this, so I breathed through it for the next minute or so. After that, the intense pain began to decrease, and a hot burning sensation intensified. You can feel the blood rushing to the area, and you can feel your brain trying to interpret these unfamiliar sensations. Is it pain? Is it pressure? Am I dying? It's an interesting experience to see your body figure it out. At the 8-minute mark, the pain continues to diminish, and you get very relaxed. By 10 minutes, you feel like you're in almost a nirvana state, even though there is still a lot of discomfort. Rolling off the mat is pretty uncomfortable, although it's manageable. Your back will be all red, and you will see 3,000 little dimple indentations from the points. It's kind of itchy afterward for a few minutes, but that all goes away shortly.

    Here's the crazy part. From the very first time I did this, my back pain has been virtually gone. All of it. The pain relief lasts almost a full day, so I do it once in the morning and again in the evening when I start to feel discomfort set back in. The only explanation I can think of is that it resets your body's pain receptors or disrupts their normal pathways to the brain or something. I'm not a doctor, but I can tell something was shifted in my brain's perception of pain signals.

    Although it's an uncomfortable 20 minutes out of my day, IT IS WORTH IT. I feel great the rest of the day, and my whole outlook on life has improved. Living in chronic pain is a difficult experience, and I am so thankful to have found something that helps get me off the pain meds and brings me much-needed relief.
